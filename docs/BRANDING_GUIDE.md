### Typography Guidelines

**Hierarchy**
- Use clear visual hierarchy with size, weight, and spacing
- Maintain consistent spacing between headings and content
- Use no more than 3 font sizes per page section

**Readability**
- Minimum body text size: 16px
- Line length: 60-75 characters (optimal reading width)
- Line height: 1.5-1.6 for body text
- Letter spacing: Default for body, slightly tighter for headings

**Accessibility**
- Ensure contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for body text
- Ensure contrast ratio of at least 3:1 for large text (18px+)
- Use semantic HTML headings (h1-h6)
- Don't rely solely on color to convey information

## ğŸ’¬ Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice

**Professional yet Approachable**
- Speak with authority but remain accessible
- Avoid jargon unless necessary, then explain it
- Be confident but not arrogant

**Clear and Direct**
- Get to the point quickly
- Use active voice
- Break down complex concepts

**Helpful and Supportive**
- Anticipate user needs
- Provide clear guidance
- Be encouraging, not condescending

**Secure and Trustworthy**
- Emphasize security without being alarmist
- Build confidence through transparency
- Use precise, accurate language

### Tone by Context

#### Marketing & Website
- **Tone:** Confident, inspiring, professional
- **Voice:** "DocuMind AI transforms how you work with documents"
- **Avoid:** Overpromising, hype language

#### Product UI & Copy
- **Tone:** Clear, helpful, concise
- **Voice:** "Upload your document to get started"
- **Avoid:** Technical jargon, unnecessary words

#### Error Messages
- **Tone:** Helpful, clear, solution-oriented
- **Voice:** "Something went wrong. Let's try again."
- **Avoid:** Blaming the user, technical error codes without explanation

#### Documentation
- **Tone:** Educational, thorough, organized
- **Voice:** "Follow these steps to upload your document"
- **Avoid:** Assumptions about user knowledge

#### Support & Help
- **Tone:** Patient, empathetic, solution-focused
- **Voice:** "We're here to help. Let's solve this together."
- **Avoid:** Dismissive language, "it's obvious" statements

### Writing Guidelines

**Do:**
- âœ… Use "you" to address users directly
- âœ… Use active voice ("Upload your document" not "Document can be uploaded")
- âœ… Break complex tasks into steps
- âœ… Use specific, concrete language
- âœ… Write for scanning (headings, bullets, short paragraphs)

**Don't:**
- âŒ Use passive voice unnecessarily
- âŒ Use jargon without explanation
- âŒ Write long, dense paragraphs
- âŒ Use vague language ("things", "stuff")
- âŒ Assume user knowledge

### Example Copy

**Good:**
> "Upload your PDF document and get instant AI-powered insights. Our secure platform analyzes your document and answers your questions with citations."

**Bad:**
> "Utilize our advanced document processing capabilities to leverage AI-driven analytical insights through our secure infrastructure."
